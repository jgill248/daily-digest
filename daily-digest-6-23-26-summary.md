# AI Daily Digest Summary — June 23, 2026

- **Z.ai's GLM-5.2 is the first open-weight model to credibly challenge closed-source frontier models on coding benchmarks**, beating GPT-5.5 on FrontierSWE at one-sixth the API cost (753B params, MIT license, $0.87/M output); Z.ai also forecasts an open-weight model matching Fable 5 capability by end of 2026.

- **China unveiled a $295 billion, five-year national AI infrastructure plan** that mandates 80% domestic chips (primarily Huawei), explicitly locking out Nvidia and AMD from the majority of its data center build-out — the most significant state-coordinated AI compute investment outside the US.

- **Adobe expanded its Creative Agent into public beta across Photoshop, Premiere Pro, Illustrator, InDesign, and Frame.io** (June 18), letting users describe desired outcomes while the agent orchestrates multi-step workflows; integrations with ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, Gemini, and Slack make Creative Cloud callable from external AI platforms.

- **PwC's 2026 AI Jobs Barometer found a 62% wage premium for AI-skilled workers** and a two-track labor market emerging: "professionalised" AI-augmented roles are growing twice as fast with 42% faster salary growth than "democratised" roles, while a Black Duck study shows 97% of enterprise developers use AI coding tools but only one-third have full governance frameworks — companies with governance are 55% more likely to report major efficiency gains.
