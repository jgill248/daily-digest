# AI Daily Digest Summary — May 13, 2026

- **OpenAI launched Daybreak, its direct answer to Anthropic's Project Glasswing.** The cybersecurity platform — built on GPT-5.5-Cyber and Codex Security — helps organizations find and patch vulnerabilities before attackers do, with major partners (Cisco, CrowdStrike, Palo Alto, Cloudflare, and others) already integrated at launch; unlike Glasswing, it's broadly accessible rather than gated.

- **Google went all-in on consumer AI at its Android Show: I/O Edition.** Gemini Intelligence debuts as a cross-app agentic layer for Android (it moves across apps and completes tasks, not just answers questions), Googlebooks are Google's first Gemini-native laptop line launching this fall with five OEM partners, and the full Google I/O (May 19–20) is expected to unveil Gemini 4.

- **The enterprise AI gap is widening in both directions.** OpenAI's B2B Signals research shows frontier-adopting firms use 3.5× more AI per employee than peers — but a Coastal report released the same week found 46% of enterprise AI initiatives are falling short, a stark reminder that deployment without workflow redesign produces little ROI.

- **Cisco's Codex implementation produced a 50% cut in code review time and compressed multi-week migrations to days** — a new large-scale case study that extends beyond the Duolingo developer-productivity story covered last week, showing AI's impact across complex, high-friction engineering work.

- **xAI Grok 4.3 and Google Gemma 4 round out the frontier picture.** Grok 4.3 (beta since April 17, not previously covered) adds native video input, 2M token context, and direct PDF/spreadsheet/slide generation; Gemma 4 is Google's most capable open-weights release and signals aggressive open-source competition ahead of the I/O frontier showcase.
