# AI Daily Digest — June 1, 2026 — Summary

- **NVIDIA launches Cosmos 3 at Computex** — an open physical-world foundation model using a mixture-of-transformers architecture that combines vision reasoning, world generation, and action prediction to generate 30-second predictive navigation videos for robots; Boston Dynamics, Caterpillar, LG, and Franka Robotics are among the first production partners.
- **NVIDIA completes a full physical AI stack** alongside Cosmos 3 with Isaac GR00T N1.7 (commercially-licensed humanoid VLA model now in early access) and Alpamayo 1.5 (reasoning VLA for autonomous vehicles), giving NVIDIA a vertically integrated platform with no direct single-vendor competitor.
- **Google retires Gemini 2.0 Flash today** (June 1), forcing all remaining users onto the Gemini 3.x family — a milestone that compresses the model generational cycle to roughly 18 months and signals how fast organizations must now plan for forced AI infrastructure upgrades.
- **Wikipedia volunteer editors are organizing a strike** after Wikimedia Foundation dissolved its Community Tech team and fired its founding CTO, even as the Foundation earns millions annually licensing Wikipedia content to AI training pipelines — a sharp illustration of the tension between AI-generated revenue and the human communities whose labor created the underlying data.
- **The AI labor conflict is broadening beyond corporations**: Wikimedia's case shows that nonprofits and volunteer-driven communities face the same dynamic as large employers — AI revenue streams built on human-generated content, followed by reduced investment in the human infrastructure that produced it.
