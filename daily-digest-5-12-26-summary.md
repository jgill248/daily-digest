# AI Daily Digest — May 12, 2026 — Summary

- **Google's Android Show (today) previewed Android 17 and Gemini's OS-level integration**, revealing app bubbles for floating multitasking, agentic AI hooks, XR smart glasses with live translation, and the Aluminium OS (a unified Android + ChromeOS platform) — all setting the stage for Google I/O next week (May 19–20), where Gemini 4 is expected.

- **OpenAI launched the Deployment Company ("DeployCo") on May 11** — a $4B+ joint venture with 19 investors including TPG, Goldman Sachs, and BBVA, plus an acquisition of Tomoro's 150 AI engineers, designed to embed OpenAI engineers directly inside enterprises and close the gap between model access and production deployment.

- **Microsoft Agent 365's May update added cross-cloud governance**: IT teams can now discover and manage AI agents running on AWS Bedrock and Google Cloud from a single dashboard, and June will bring shadow-AI detection for unmanaged agents (including Claude Code) running on Windows devices.

- **IBM's 2026 CEO survey found 76% of companies now have a Chief AI Officer** — up from 26% just a year ago — and that 93% cite culture (not technology) as the #1 barrier to AI adoption, with only 25% of employees using AI regularly despite most CEOs believing their workforce is AI-ready.

- **The AI productivity paradox is the defining enterprise challenge of 2026**: 66% of organizations see individual productivity gains from AI, but only 29% achieve significant organizational ROI — the gap comes down to workflow redesign, and the companies in that 29% are nearly 3x more likely to have fundamentally restructured processes around AI rather than bolted it on top.
